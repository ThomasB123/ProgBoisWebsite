The Holy Trinity,

Durham has a full total of seven clubs spread accross the city.
Here I talk about my 3 favourites,

Fabios (Fabs)

Smoking area 3/5 – “Rooftop Terrace Well Thought Out”
A curious one, you end up climbing what seems to be an infinite number of stairs and once you’re there it almost feels as if you’re pressured to stay dreading the descent. I reckon that’s how people who climb Everest feel; fluorescent lighting tend to expose stains most people should never see.

Jimmy Allens
(Jimmies)

Smoking area 4/5 –“Not exactly nice, but probably the most sociable place in Durham at 2am”
Who doesn’t like being rammed into what is essentially someone’s living room under a bridge. Granted, its free and who doesn’t like a good mosh pit with a fight ensuing at any moment. The music tends to appeal to a crowd of typically students who often know not the lyrics, but refuse to admit that they’ve drank their body weight in Aldi cider and they’re not actually having any fun

Klute

Smoking area 2/5 – “Avoid At All Costs”
Now I was told REPEATEDLY that this was the worst club in Europe, as far as I can tell, its an average club with clearly watered-down spirits sold for pennies on the pound. The patrons are generally unenthusiastic, who could blame them? That glorified shed could fall into the river at any given point; it is not uncommon then to find the ‘club’ goers seek refuge in the ‘smoking area’; which by the way; resembles more of a septic tank than I care for.

Bar Crawls.

I find the term bar crawl fitting, this is often because these nights out are very poorly thought out.
One night, we decided to plan a tour of all the bars that are remotely worth visiting; we didn’t stop there…
this is the most time and effort efficient way to traverse our various student only pubs.




  • Grey College

    Chili vodka

    What is easily the most disgusting college drink, the ghost pepper syrup feels like a satanic infestation of your esophagus. Bar feels like a local pub in Sheringham, don’t go on darts night!

  • Collingwood

    Red and Black

    Bar is often crowded; sporty people aren’t exactly my cup of tea… they seem friendly though.

  • Trevelyan

    Trev’s Blue

    Looks and tastes like Windex on ice. Beware the hexagons, bar is quite spacious; often has live ‘music’.

  • St Aiden’s

    The Badger

    Guinness and VK, an interesting combination that really helps with the various carcinogens in trevs drink. Bar is nice but nothing special.

  • St Mary’s

    The Witch

    Fruity shot of an unknown spirit, ignorance is truly bliss. Mary’s has a bad rep for the dungeon like bar, to be fair at this point; you might not want to spend too much time here, there’s 5 colleges to go.

  • St Cuthbert’s

    Brownie bomber

    Beer + Syrup = Lazy. Cuths does have another bar on Whinney hill; by the time you’ve arrived you’re probably glad to be on the Bailey, the castle is in sight.

  • St Johns

    The Tripod

    Three flavours of VK, 4% ABV at this point is just about all you can take with 3 bars left. Bar is no swan, but then at this point they all feel the same.

  • St Chads

    The Goat

    Green concoction with an unlabeled spirit and detergent like flavour. The literal glass ceiling is the only reason anyone ever comes here. Very classy bar, one of the best.

  • Hatfield

    The Hatfield Tickler

    Weak, just like your legs and liver at this point; granted its quite sweet. Bar conjures images of a Gatwick Weatherspoon’s.

  • Castle

    Smernergy

    A nice combo of Smirnoff ice, vodka and red bull, which sadly I did not opt for, but rather a salted caramel toastie from their very elegant toasty bar. Every thing in castle is over-done, formals are pretty much every night of the week. They serve Rekorderlig on tap. A very nice bar that you will need to surrender your campus card to enter.
